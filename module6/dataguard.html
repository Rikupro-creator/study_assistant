📐 Physical standby architecture – component flow

LNS (Log Network Server) · RFS (Remote File/Fetch Service) · MRP (Managed Recovery Process) · SRL (Standby Redo Logs)

🔹 Primary side processes

  • LGWR – writes redo to online redo logs (cyclic).
  • LNS (log network server) – reads redo buffer (or falls back to online redo if behind) and ships over Oracle Net.
  • ARCn – archiver, creates archivelogs, used by RFS for gap resolution.

🔸 Standby side processes

  • RFS (remote file/fetch service) – receives redo from LNS, writes to standby redo logs (SRL). Can fetch missing archivelogs (gap resolution).
  • MRP (managed recovery process) – applies redo from SRL to standby database.
⚡ LNS fallback: If LNS cannot keep up with LGWR, it reads directly from online redo logs (dashed gold arrow).
⚡ Gap resolution: When standby detects missing logs, RFS contacts primary ARCn and pulls archivelogs automatically (red dotted line) — but if RMAN deleted them, manual intervention is needed.

📚 Standby redo logs (SRL) vs. online redo logs

📌 Online redo logs (primary & standby)

  • Cyclic, written by LGWR.
  • On standby: remain idle until role switch (standby becomes primary).
  • Not used for media recovery while in standby role.

📌 Standby redo logs (SRL)

  • Special logs created ALTER DATABASE ADD STANDBY LOGFILE.
  • RFS writes incoming redo directly to SRL.
  • MRP reads SRL to apply changes.
  • Must exist on both primary and standby for seamless role transition (switchover/failover).
⚠️ Interview tip: Many DBAs forget SRL on primary. After failover, the new primary (old standby) expects SRL on the demoted primary. Without SRL, you cannot perform a clean switchover.

⚠️ RMAN-08137: WARNING – archived log not deleted

RMAN-08137: WARNING: archived log not deleted, needed for standby or upstream capture process

RMAN refuses to delete archivelogs because they are still required by a Data Guard standby, GoldenGate extract, or an obsolete capture process. If ignored, archive log destinations fill up and databases may hang.

🔍 Why does this happen (even without Data Guard/GoldenGate)?

  • Orphaned capture processes remain registered in DBA_CAPTURE from old replication.
  • Deletion policy set to APPLIED ON STANDBY or SHIPPED TO STANDBY but standby is down or lagging.
  • Archive gap – standby missing logs; they are preserved automatically.

🛠️ METHOD 1: Check archive dependencies & policy

-- 1. Detect any archive gap between primary and standby
SELECT THREAD#, LOW_SEQUENCE#, HIGH_SEQUENCE# FROM V$ARCHIVE_GAP;

-- 2. Which archived logs are still not deleted?
SELECT SEQUENCE#, FIRST_TIME, DELETED FROM V$ARCHIVED_LOG WHERE DELETED='NO';

-- 3. Show current RMAN archivelog deletion policy
RMAN> SHOW ALL;

If policy is too strict (e.g., APPLIED ON STANDBY), temporarily relax it:

RMAN> CONFIGURE ARCHIVELOG DELETION POLICY TO SHIPPED TO STANDBY;
RMAN> DELETE NOPROMPT ARCHIVELOG ALL COMPLETED BEFORE 'SYSDATE-1';
-- restore later
RMAN> CONFIGURE ARCHIVELOG DELETION POLICY TO NONE;

🔄 METHOD 2: Unused capture processes & standby lag

-- List any capture processes (GoldenGate/Streams)
SELECT CAPTURE_NAME, STATUS FROM DBA_CAPTURE;

-- If unused, unregister (GoldenGate example)
GGSCI> DBLOGIN USERIDALIAS <alias>
GGSCI> UNREGISTER EXTRACT <extract_name> DATABASE

-- Check Data Guard apply status
SELECT PROCESS, STATUS, SEQUENCE# FROM V$MANAGED_STANDBY WHERE PROCESS IN ('MRP0','RFS');

-- Verify logs applied on standby destinations
SELECT DEST_ID, APPLIED_SCN FROM V$ARCHIVE_DEST_STATUS WHERE STATUS='VALID';

If standby is lagging, wait until MRP catches up or troubleshoot network/recovery.

📈 METHOD 3: Proactive prevention

  • Monitor free space in fast recovery area:
    SELECT name, space_limit - space_used AS free_mb FROM V$RECOVERY_FILE_DEST;
  • Set up alerts when apply lag exceeds threshold (e.g., using Enterprise Manager or custom scripts).
  • Audit registered capture processes monthly – remove obsolete entries.
  • In 11g/12c and later, use CONFIGURE ARCHIVELOG DELETION POLICY TO APPLIED ON ALL STANDBY after ensuring all standbys are healthy.
📌 Best practice: Integrate archive deletion policy with backup retention; ensure it aligns with standby requirements. Use BACKUP ... DELETE INPUT carefully.

🧩 Gap resolution – when automation fails

RFS automatically fetches missing archivelogs from primary if they still exist on disk. But if RMAN or a backup job deleted the necessary archivelogs, you must intervene:

  1. Identify gap sequence:
    SELECT * FROM V$ARCHIVE_GAP;
  2. Restore missing logs from backup to primary (or copy from another source).
  3. Register them via ALTER DATABASE REGISTER LOGFILE '/path/...'.
  4. RFS will detect and ship them automatically.
❗ Common cause of RMAN-08137: Archive logs are deleted on primary before being shipped/applied on standby. Tune backup scripts with:
BACKUP ARCHIVELOG ALL NOT BACKED UP 1 TIMES DELETE INPUT; and ensure deletion policy includes APPLIED ON STANDBY.

🎤 Interview / real-world Q&A

  • Q: Does standby need its own redo logs? Why SRL?
    A: Yes, SRL separate from online redo. If standby is open (active Data Guard) it generates its own redo. SRL keeps incoming changes isolated.
  • Q: What if I don't configure SRL on primary?
    A: Switchover will fail; primary cannot become a standby without SRL.
  • Q: Why does RMAN-08137 appear even if I have no standby?
    A: Check for orphaned capture processes in DBA_CAPTURE, or a previously registered Data Guard broker configuration.
  • Q: Can I force delete logs causing RMAN-08137?
    A: Only after confirming no replication/standby requires them, or after disabling the capture/unregistering. Use DELETE ARCHIVELOG ... FORCE as last resort, but risk breaking DR.
